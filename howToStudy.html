Study Materials for Natural Science written exams

Jan 10

When I decided to aim for the MEXT Undergraduate scholarship, the 'syllabus' for the written exams was nothing but an enigma to me. MEXT scholarship is pretty niche, so finding resources specific to it is rare. So after getting the scholarship, like a good senpai, I am sharing my experience and study materials for the exams with my potential kouhais.

Think about this, MEXT expects the scholars to study in Japanese Universities alongside Japanese students. That would imply- that we must have academic knowledge greater than or at least equal to an average Japanese high school student. So it seems obvious we must learn the same things as a Japanese high school student would. What better resource to do that than Japanese high school books! Right? Well...partly.

If you are anything like the average Natural Science applicant, your Japanese proficiency is not high enough to read textbooks in Japanese. Now you could invest your time honing your Japanese up to that level, but it seems counterintuitive to spend more time on Japanese than 'Natural Science' subjects, i.e., Physics, Mathematics, Chemistry. Please do not mistake this as me saying learning Japanese is irrelevant. Quite the opposite. You must learn Japanese but on the side. Remember, for Natural Science, Japanese proficiency is a preferred quality, not a required one.

In that case, we must find a resource equivalent to that in a language we already know. For a lot of us, it's English. Turns out the syllabus taught to high school students in 'India' is very similar to the Japanese high school syllabus. Strictly studying the high school syllabus will place you near a 50% score. We must aim higher. We must strive for >=80% to improve our chances of getting the scholarship. So I have modified it a bit. Replaced the physics textbook with a high-level book and added resources for solving previous year's problems from an Indian university entrance exam. The ideal time required for reading the theory and solving problems in the textbooks is about 2 years. But it can be done within one year as well.

Most students find the Math MEXT exam difficult. So, we must spend relatively more time on Math. Also, sometimes, the questions asked in Chemistry are entirely factual and hard to find in textbooks. Do not worry about them. Since we cannot fully prepare for Chemistry, the best course of action is to emphasize more on Physics.

So the time you must invest in each subject should be as follows, Math> Physics> Chemistry> Japanese

Methodology

  1. General Approach and Attitude:

    2. Read the theory given at the start of the chapter. Keep in mind the definitions and formulae. The books will explain the thought process to reach conclusions. Make sure you follow the explanations. A frequent trap people fall into is- feeling the need to COMPLETELY understand the theory before solving the problems. You are never truly ready. Just jump into the problems once you have a minimum understanding of the theory. Try actively recalling the theory while solving. That way, you remember things longer. It's also common to fall into the thinking that 'I must solve the current problem to proceed to the next problem.' This attitude will only waste your time. Move on.

    You must study everyday. There is no way around it. I advice that you study every subject daily instead of doing one subject for the entire day. That way you won't be bored XD

  2. Solve problems in 'waves':

    3. Look through every problem and mark the ones you feel you can quickly solve. Solve those first. This completes the first wave. Now look through the remaining problems and find problems you can 'maybe' solve if you struggle a bit. Now solve those. This completes the second wave. Keep doing this process until problems you find 'impossible' to solve remain. It's okay to look up solutions for these on the internet.

  3. Get acquainted with as many 'problem types' as possible:

    4. No textbook reading will help you if you don't know 'problem-solving techniques.' Note the formulae that are frequently asked. Mentally categorizing problems based on given data and asked data is helpful. In mathematics, it's common to use some 'tricks' to simplify the expressions. Whenever you encounter such 'tricks,' remember them.

Solving previous year papers for MEXT exams and JEE Mains

After completing the textbooks, it is time for more intense problem-solving. JEE mains is one of the university entrance exams in India that I find is close to MEXT exams. The past papers with solutions are readily available on the internet.

Each paper has 3 sections Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. There will be some out of syllabus questions in the papers. (The chapters we skipped in Physics.) Just skip them. They are irrelevant to us. You are supposed to solve each section in 1 hour. Aim for that. Try to score as high as possible. After the time limit, if you get something wrong or have no idea how to solve it, take a look at the solutions. Try to understand the 'algorithms' used. After solving enough problems, you will remember these 'algorithms' for common problem types. You can finish solving 5 papers in 5 days.

Now it's time to move on to MEXT's previous year papers. Solve the papers in the order - recent to old. The recent ones are differently structured compared to the older ones. You must aim to solve all of them. That's 3 hrs per day, which is doable.

If you still have time after doing all that... you are a productivity expert.
